Corruption in Police Sector

Why is Corruption in the Malaysian Police Force So Common?

Young Man Arrested for Attempted Bribery at Roadblock

Nilai , July 26, 2024 : A young man was apprehended yesterday at a routine roadblock for attempting to bribe a police officer. The incident occurred when the driver was stopped for not wearing a seatbelt.

Upon being informed that he would receive a ticket for the violation, the man allegedly offered a sum of money to the officer in an attempt to avoid the penalty. The officer, adhering to the strict anti-corruption policies in place, immediately rejected the bribe and placed the man under arrest.

Authorities have charged the individual with attempting to corrupt a public servant, a serious offense under Malaysian law. This incident highlights ongoing efforts to combat corruption within the nation's law enforcement and uphold the rule of law.

Introduction

Corruption is a serious and long-standing problem in Malaysia. It not only affects politics, business, and education but also affects Malaysia's reputation. Due to corruption cases in Malaysia, foreign tourists may have doubts about Malaysia's security and fairness. It led to a decrease in tourist and tourism industry revenue. Although the Malaysia government and the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) are working hard to crack down on criminals of corruption, corruption still exists in Malaysia. The 1MDB scandal is an example of misusing billions of dollars of state funds and involving top officials in extensive police corruption, demonstrating the seriousness of the issue. The corrupt behavior of senior managers erodes the public's trust in government agencies and hinders fair competition in the market. This has affected the country's economic growth and social stability. Despite its deep-rooted nature, the problem of corruption can be mitigated through the concerted efforts of all sectors of society and continuous reforms.



Research Background

Police corruption in Malaysia is a well-known problem involving illegal activities such as bribery, solicitation, and abuse of power. In Malaysia, police corruption is noticeable in different criminal activities such as the discreet request for bribes, commonly referred to as "coffee money". Therefore, individuals are able to avoid issuing tickets through this type of behavior. Law enforcement officers abused their powers and accepted bribes to show leniency when dealing with crimes. This behavior leads the public to think that money can solve everything including bypassing the constraints and sanctions of the law, thereby weakening the binding and deterrent effect of the law. Although the government and different organizations try to solve and strengthen these actions such as imposing more severe punishment, the Malaysian police still cannot be associated with integrity. The root cause of police corruption is not only moral flaws but also the career and financial status of the police is in question. This has resulted in some police officers using illegal ways to earn extra income. Hence, we will explore and study what causes police bribery and the impact of this behavior in Malaysia.

Research Methodology

Research methodology is a technique and procedure used to identify and analyse information on a specific research topic to ensure that the research results are valid and reliable (Sreekumar, 2023).

In this study, we employ content analysis to investigate police corruption. The purpose is to understand the causes, information and effects of police corruption. We analyzed a range of sources including news reports, journals and books. We also identified relevant literature by searching for keywords such as “police” and “causes of corruption”. A preliminary review via malaysiamail shows that Malaysia's Corruption Perception Index (CPI) score for 2022 was 47 which is the lowest in a decade that prompting a review of police corruption in the country.

Channel News Asia reports also show that in the past ten years, more than 1,600 police officers in Malaysia have been dismissed due to crimes which shows that there are systemic problems. Abramson & Denenberg, P.C., a professional law firm also states that factors that contribute to police corruption include a lack of accountability, low wages and financial pressures, inadequate training and systemic problems. Reports and case studies show police corruption threatens social security, increases the cost of public services and has a negative impact on the tourism industry through police extortion of tourists as mentioned in the New Straits Times report.

Overall, our content analysis provides insight into the serious problem of police corruption in Malaysia and highlights the need for effective measures to combat corruption to maintain a fair society.

Research Finding

Lack of accountability and oversight

Police corruption in Malaysia is rooted in poor oversight and weak accountability mechanisms. It has led police officers to believe that they can engage in corrupt activities with impunity. Weak internal controls, ineffective disciplinary actions, and the absence of an independent oversight body exacerbate the problem. The lack of strong oversight allows corruption to flourish because they will face no significant consequences or even their actions will be difficult to detect by relevant authorities. Therefore, they grow bolder. According to Transparency International, the Corruption Perceptions Index 2022 in Malaysia is score of 47 out of 100 (CPI 2022). It is the most severe in ten years and highlights the seriousness of the issue. The combination of these factors has led to the aggravation of the problem of corruption. It emphasizes the need for more effective oversight and accountability measures. (Razak, 2023)

Low wages and economic pressure

Police salaries are often insufficient to support their families or meet their financial needs, which forces some to resort to corrupt practices, such as accepting bribes or engaging in illegal activities to supplement their income. The economic difficulties faced by police officers increase their temptation to seek quick financial gains through corrupt means, thus creating a cycle of corruption. The highly stressful nature of police work, coupled with feelings of underpayment, undervaluing and disrespect from the communities they serve, may lead police officers to view corruption as a form of compensation for their efforts and risks. (Denenberg, 2021) In addition, in an environment where corruption is prevalent, police officers may perceive corrupt behaviour as normal or justified. This perception is further exacerbated by a culture within the police department that either passively tolerates corruption or actively promotes it. When police officers see their colleagues engaging in corrupt activities without consequence, they may feel that such behaviour is acceptable or even expected. Addressing financial pressures, improving working conditions, increasing salaries, providing adequate support and fostering a culture of integrity within the police force are key steps in combating police corruption in Malaysia.

Inadequate training and systemic factors

Inadequate training and systemic factors play a significant role in police corruption in Malaysia. Systemic issues such as a lack of ethics training, poor leadership, and a culture that values loyalty over integrity all contribute to the rise of corrupt behavior. New police officers often feel pressure to abide by these norms to adapt and advance their careers (Denenberg, 2021). Police departments may fail to provide adequate training to prevent corruption, misconduct and violence. New police officers often lack institutionalized moral training. It makes them unaware of their moral and legal obligations and the punishment for participating in corruption. When police corruption is obvious, it usually shows that the police department either condones the culture of corruption or turns a blind eye to it. New police officers may perceive such behavior as the norm and act accordingly. It will perpetuate the cycle of corruption. These systemic problems are solved through comprehensive moral training, strong leadership and a culture that emphasizes honesty.

Discussion

Threat to social security

Police corruption poses a serious threat to the security of society. Police may choose selectively enforce the law in favor of individuals who offer bribes. According to the South China Morning Post, Malaysian police allegedly accepted cash from a British couple to pay for a traffic ticket on a road linking Penang and the Cameron Highlands (Malaysia Police Seek British “van Life” Couple over Traffic Stop Bribery Video, 2024). The alleged police officer in this incident gave the couple two options either to pay a RM300 fine or pay RM100 on the spot. This event shows how bribery enables people to avoid facing legal repercussions. In contrast, individuals who are unable to afford or unwilling to give bribes may face serious legal consequences. Instead of combating crime, a corrupt police force protects criminals and enables criminal behavior to thrive. Police cover-ups enable criminals to continue their illegal activities. It leads to a steady rise in crime, which directly threatens the safety of the public's lives and property. Society's peace and stability are also threatened (Oris I. Jaen, n.d.).

Increased cost of public services

Dr. Budi Widodo who is a legal expert and senior researcher at LIPI emphasized the effects of police corruption on public services. Corruption within the police force bring negatively impacts to a rise in the expenses of public services (Wibison et al., 2024). Police officers spend their time and effort on taking bribes instead of concentrating on law enforcement, leading to the misuse of police resources. Police resources that should be used to maintain public safety and combat crime are wasted on personal benefit. Some police officers may opt to stay quiet and not reveal the unethical actions of their fellow officers (Police Corruption and Police Reforms in Developing Societies: Hope Sr., Kempe Ronald: 9781498731874: Amazon.com: Books, 2024). This has weakened the capacity of law enforcement agencies and led to a significant drop in the efficiency of policing work. Due to police corruption, society's need for and spending on maintaining law enforcement has risen. In order to bridge the gap, the government must allocate additional funds to expand the police presence (Singh, 2022). Therefore, the increase in public services expenses directly affects the country's budget and economic growth.

Impact on the Tourism Sector

According to the New Straits Times, Langkawi police are investigating an incident where police officers demanded RM100 from tourists for not wearing crash helmets on 30 January 2024 (Mukhtar, 2024). This kind of corrupt behavior has seriously jeopardized the country's tourism industry. When tourists experience extortion and unfair treatment by the police, they feel unsafe and develop a negative view of the country. These negative experiences can quickly spread through social media. This could increase the effect and possibly result in a major decrease in tourist numbers. (Das & Dirienzo, 2010). Foreign tourists are a crucial source of income for many countries. A decrease in tourism has a direct impact on industries like restaurants, hotels, transportation and so on (The Herald, 2017). Not only that, police corruption once widely reported, it can seriously damage the country's international image and reputation. In the long run, this situation will also lead to a slowdown in the overall economic development of the country (Akamavi et al., 2022).

Conclusion

The founder of Transparency International, Peter Eigen has said that people need to be aware that they have the power to transform a corrupt system (Transparency Int’l, 2018). This one belief emphasizes the importance of individuals or groups in driving systemic change. As a Malaysian citizen, we need to unite preventing corrupt practices to safeguard the well-being of our society and economy. In order to prevent corruption from entering into a deadly cycle, we should act proactively. Therefore, the government can establish a system for reporting corrupt activities anonymously. It is to protect whistleblowers and encourage more people to expose corrupt conduct. Malaysian people are encouraged to participate in combating corruption to help decrease the country's crime rate. We must collaborate as a team to eliminate corruption. By working together, we can forge a more optimistic future for our nation.
