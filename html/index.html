Corruption in Police Sector

Why is Corruption in the Malaysian Police Force So Common?

Young Man Arrested for Attempted Bribery at Roadblock

Nilai , July 26, 2024 : A young man was apprehended yesterday at a routine roadblock for attempting to bribe a police officer. The incident occurred when the driver was stopped for not wearing a seatbelt.

Upon being informed that he would receive a ticket for the violation, the man allegedly offered a sum of money to the officer in an attempt to avoid the penalty. The officer, adhering to the strict anti-corruption policies in place, immediately rejected the bribe and placed the man under arrest.

Authorities have charged the individual with attempting to corrupt a public servant, a serious offense under Malaysian law. This incident highlights ongoing efforts to combat corruption within the nation's law enforcement and uphold the rule of law.

Introduction

Corruption is a long-standing and serious problem in Malaysia. It affects all areas of politics, business and education, and it has also affected Malaysia's reputation. Although the Malaysia government and anti-corruption agencies such as the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) are working hard to crack down on criminals and educate the public on the seriousness of corruption. But corruption still exists in Malaysia, from the 1MDB scandal to police corruption, which highlights the seriousness of the problem. Despite its deep-rooted nature, the problem of corruption can be mitigated through sustained joint efforts and continuous reforms by all sectors of society.



Research Background

Police corruption in Malaysia is a long-standing and well-known problem involving illegal activities such as bribery, solicitation of bribes and abuse of power. The Malaysia police are most often perceived to be implicitly soliciting bribes from people on the road in order not to issue tickets, which has come to be known as "coffee money". The publicized scandals have captured the attention and resonance of the public, which has greatly affected the public's confidence in the law enforcement agencies. Despite the efforts of the government and various agencies to combat and improve these behaviors, the Malaysia police still cannot be associated with integrity. However, there is a segment of the population that believes that there is a reason for this, and the reasons for the corruption of the Malaysia police will be explored in this assignment.

We analyzed a range of sources including news reports, journals and books. We also identified relevant literature by searching for keywords such as âpoliceâ and âcauses of corruptionâ. Channel News Asia reports also show that in the past ten years, more than 1,600 police officers in Malaysia have been dismissed due to crimes which shows that there are systemic problems. Overall, our content analysis provides insight into the serious problem of police corruption in Malaysia and highlights the need for effective measures to combat corruption to maintain a fair society.

Research Finding

1. Lack of accountability and oversight

Police corruption in Malaysia is rooted in poor oversight and weak accountability mechanisms, which have led to police officers believing that they can engage in corrupt activities with impunity. Weak internal controls, ineffective disciplinary actions and the absence of an independent oversight body exacerbate the problem. The lack of strong oversight allows corrupt practices to flourish unchecked, as police officers believe they will not face significant consequences. The severity of the problem is underscored by Malaysia's score of 47 out of 100 on the Corruption Perceptions Index 2022 (CPI 2022), the worst in a decade, according to Transparency International. Weak internal controls and ineffective disciplinary measures further contribute to the growth of corruption, highlighting the urgent need to strengthen accountability and oversight mechanisms to combat the problem. (Razak, 2023)

2. Low wages and economic pressure

Police salaries are often insufficient to support their families or meet their financial needs, which forces some to resort to corrupt practices, such as accepting bribes or engaging in illegal activities to supplement their income. The economic difficulties faced by police officers increase their temptation to seek quick financial gains through corrupt means, thus creating a cycle of corruption. The highly stressful nature of police work, coupled with feelings of underpayment, undervaluing and disrespect from the communities they serve, may lead police officers to view corruption as a form of compensation for their efforts and risks. (Denenberg, 2021) In addition, in an environment where corruption is prevalent, police officers may perceive corrupt behaviour as normal or justified. This perception is further exacerbated by a culture within the police department that either passively tolerates corruption or actively promotes it. When police officers see their colleagues engaging in corrupt activities without consequence, they may feel that such behaviour is acceptable or even expected. Addressing financial pressures, improving working conditions, increasing salaries, providing adequate support and fostering a culture of integrity within the police force are key steps in combating police corruption in Malaysia.

3. Inadequate training and systemic factors

Inadequate training and systemic factors play a significant role in police corruption in Malaysia. Systemic problems such as lack of ethics training, poor leadership and a culture that values loyalty over integrity contribute to corrupt practices. New police officers often feel pressured to conform to these norms to fit in and advance in their careers. (Denenberg, 2021) In many cases, corrupt police officers are aware of their misconduct but believe that they will not be caught because of inadequate oversight. Police departments may fail to provide adequate training to prevent corruption, misconduct and violence. Specifically, new police officers often lack institutionalized ethics training, leaving them unaware of their ethical and legal obligations and the penalties for engaging in corruption. (Denenberg, 2021) This lack of training serves as a deterrent to ethical behaviour rather than to corruption. When serious police corruption is evident, it is usually an indication that the police department is either condoning a culture of corruption or turning a blind eye to it. New police officers may perceive such behaviour as the norm and act, accordingly, perpetuating the cycle of corruption. Addressing these systemic issues through comprehensive ethics training, strong leadership, and a culture that emphasizes integrity is critical to combating police corruption in Malaysia

Discussion

Threat to social security

Police corruption poses a serious threat to the security of society. Police may choose selectively enforce the law in favor of individuals who offer bribes. According to the South China Morning Post, Malaysian police allegedly accepted cash from a British couple to pay for a traffic ticket on a road linking Penang and the Cameron Highlands (Malaysia Police Seek British âvan Lifeâ Couple over Traffic Stop Bribery Video, 2024). The alleged police officer in this incident gave the couple two options either to pay a RM300 fine or pay RM100 on the spot. This event shows how bribery enables people to avoid facing legal repercussions. In contrast, individuals who are unable to afford or unwilling to give bribes may face serious legal consequences. Instead of combating crime, a corrupt police force protects criminals and enables criminal behavior to thrive. Police cover-ups enable criminals to continue their illegal activities. It leads to a steady rise in crime, which directly threatens the safety of public's lives and property. Society's peace and stability are also threatened (Oris I. Jaen, n.d.).

Impact on the Tourism Sector

According to the New Straits Times, Langkawi police are investigating an incident where police officers demanded RM100 from tourists for not wearing crash helmets on 30 January 2024 (Mukhtar, 2024). This kind of corrupt behaviour has seriously jeopardised the country's tourism industry. When tourists experience extortion and unfair treatment by the police, they feel unsafe and develop a negative view of the country. These negative experiences can quickly spread through social media. This will amplify the impact and potentially leading to a significant loss of the tourist numbers (Das & Dirienzo, 2010). Foreign tourists are a crucial source of income for many countries A decrease in tourism has a direct impact on industries like restaurants, hotels, transportation and so on (The Herald, 2017) . Not only that, police corruption once widely reported, it can seriously damage the country's international image and reputation. In the long run, this situation will also lead to a slowdown in overall economic development of the country (Akamavi et al., 2022).
